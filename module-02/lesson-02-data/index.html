Orpheus the Cartographer

"To you this tale refers, Who seek to lead your mind Into the upper day; For he who overcome should turn back his gaze Towards the Tartarean cave, Whatever excellence he takes with him He loses when he looks on those below." - Orpheus's Gaze, Boethius

Pickles reminds us that while we are cartographers, we are still mere mortals. We may believe our representations of the world (or any part therein) may be "True" - we must be aware of our limitations and biases. While we look down on the world with the aid of our beautifully crafted map utilizing the best scientific techniques at our disposal, we must recognize the imperfections of our methods, both technical and artistic.
