Orpheus the Cartographer

Here is an aesthetically pleasing (but difficult to read) representation of the world.

"To you this tale refers, Who seek to lead your mind Into the upper day; For he who overcome should turn back his gaze Towards the Tartarean cave, Whatever excellence he takes with him He loses when he looks on those below." - Orpheus's Gaze, Boethius

Pickles reminds us that while we are cartographers, we are still mere mortals. We may feel like our representations the world (or any part therein) may be "True" - we must be aware of our limitations and biases.
